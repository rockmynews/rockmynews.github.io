There is a ship, Ship of Theseus. After each voyage, one plank of wood is replaced. After 12 years, all the planks have been replaced. What we have now is nothing of what was there in the beginning.

Is it still the Ship of Theseus? It can't be. Nothing that was there still is. But we think it still is. 

What then makes the ship, Ship of Theseus? Clearly not the planks. But is the ship nothing but the planks?

But it still is the Ship of Theseus. How? What makes it so?

When one changes, what makes them the former self? Is the person the same person just because they carry the same name and resembles the old self? Some people change visually. A lot of them. So it really is a glimmer of resemblance. Plus the name.

So is the name what makes the old one and the new one the same? Is that what makes the Ship of Theseus the Ship of Theseus after everything has changed?

Some people change even their name. But we don't consider them new. We identify them as their former self. What do we identify them with? Their habits? Habits can change. Their behavior? Behavior can change. Their attitude? Attitude can change.

What is that which was there and still is which makes one identify the other as the same?

It is such that there is something constant, that never changes, which makes us automatically identify people regardless of their present with their former self?

Clearly not. We do find some people who we would be happy to be with, we are not. And some who we would not be happy to be with, we are.


One constant, one variable, easy to deal with. Two variables, becomes difficult.

What is the new self bound by. Former words? Actions? Inactions? If yes, repentance is meaningless. If no, what happens to those? They as a part of memory still guide us. Knowingly unknowingly. Consciously unconsciously.

How does one goes from a state of affinity to aversion? Preference to indifference.
